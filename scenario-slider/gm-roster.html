Walk through managing your team roster in General Manager.

Scenario: It's the start of a new academic year. Walk through reviewing your roster in GM, making a position correction, and cleaning up an athlete who is no longer with your program.

Step 1 of 5
Navigate to the Roster tab in General Manager.
From the GM navigation, select Roster. This page shows all active athletes across your academic years and budgets in one centralized view — no need to open individual budgets to find athlete records.
Step 2 of 5
Review the roster for accuracy.
Scan the athlete list for names, positions, and headshots. GM syncs this data from Teamworks Hub — if anything looks outdated or incorrect, the fix needs to be made in Hub first, then it will sync back into GM automatically.
Step 3 of 5
Open an athlete's three-dot menu.
Find the athlete you need to update and select the three-dot icon next to their name. This reveals the roster management options available for that athlete, including removal and PSA linking.
Step 4 of 5
Remove an athlete who is no longer on your program.
Select Delete Athlete from the three-dot menu. This permanently removes the athlete from your GM roster across current and future budgets. Use this only when an athlete has left your program — not for position changes or year transitions.
Step 5 of 5
Link a committed recruit to their Hub account.
If a prospective student-athlete has committed and been added to Hub, use the Roster tab to link their GM record to their Hub account. This ensures their profile is properly connected before they become an active roster member in your budgets.

✓ Roster reviewed and updated — your GM roster now reflects your current program accurately.
