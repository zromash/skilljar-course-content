Walk through building a new budget in General Manager from start to finish.

Scenario: You're setting up a multi-year scholarship budget for your program. Walk through creating the budget, configuring your NIL values, adding categories, and getting it ready to allocate dollars to athletes.

Step 1 of 6
Go to Budgets and click New Budget.
From the GM navigation, select Budgets, then click New Budget. Enter a budget name and a start date. Choose whether this is a single-year budget or a multi-year budget — multi-year lets you view athlete commitments side by side across academic years.
Step 2 of 6
Enter your NIL values.
Your sport auto-populates based on your account. Enter the values for institutional NIL scholarships and third-party NIL. These define the total resources available in this budget. Click Create Budget to save — you can edit these values at any time.
Step 3 of 6
Set the budget status to Active.
After the budget is created, set its status to Active. This signals that the budget is live and you're ready to begin committing dollars from it to athletes. A budget can remain in draft status while you're still configuring it.
Step 4 of 6
Add categories to organize your budget.
Categories work like columns in a spreadsheet. Click Add Category, give it a name (e.g. "Tuition", "Room & Board"), and decide whether it spans multiple years in a multi-year budget. Each category can be set to include or exclude from the overall budget total.
Step 5 of 6
Use the Filter tab to view a single academic year.
In a multi-year budget, the Filter tab lets you narrow the view to a single academic year — useful when you want to focus on one year's allocation without seeing the full multi-year breakdown.
Step 6 of 6
Begin allocating dollars to athletes.
With categories set up and the budget Active, you're ready to allocate. Select an athlete in the budget and enter dollar amounts per category. You can allocate to current roster members, or import recruits from C+R to model commitment scenarios.

✓ Budget created and ready — categories configured and dollars can now be allocated to athletes.
